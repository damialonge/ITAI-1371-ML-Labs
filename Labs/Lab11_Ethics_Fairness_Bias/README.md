# Lab 11 – Ethics, Fairness & Bias in ML

## Overview
This lab examined the ethical responsibilities that come with building machine learning systems — focusing on bias, fairness, and the real-world consequences of ML decisions.

## What I Learned
- How **bias** can enter ML systems (through biased training data, feature selection, etc.)
- Types of fairness: **individual fairness** vs. **group fairness**
- Real-world examples where biased ML caused harm (hiring tools, facial recognition, criminal sentencing)
- Techniques to **detect and mitigate bias** in datasets and models
- The importance of **transparency and explainability** in ML systems
- Ethical frameworks for responsible AI development

## Challenges Faced
This lab required thinking critically rather than just technically. Understanding that "accurate" models can still be unfair — and navigating what "fair" even means across different definitions — was a genuinely difficult but important challenge.

## Why This Matters to Me
As someone in cybersecurity and IT, I see how biased or opaque ML systems can create security and trust risks in organizations. Responsible AI development is not optional — it's essential.
